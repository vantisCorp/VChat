## 📋 Our Pledge

We as members, contributors, and leaders pledge to make participation in our
community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body
size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender
identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status,
nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity
and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming,
diverse, inclusive, and healthy community.

---

## 📜 Our Standards

### Positive Behaviors

We expect all community members to:

- ✅ **Be Respectful**: Treat others with dignity and respect
- ✅ **Be Inclusive**: Welcome newcomers and diverse perspectives
- ✅ **Be Collaborative**: Work together constructively
- ✅ **Be Empathetic**: Consider others' feelings and experiences
- ✅ **Be Professional**: Maintain professional conduct
- ✅ **Be Constructive**: Provide helpful, actionable feedback
- ✅ **Be Open**: Listen to different viewpoints
- ✅ **Be Patient**: Understand that everyone learns at their own pace

### Unacceptable Behaviors

We do not tolerate:

- ❌ **Harassment**: Unwanted comments, advances, or conduct
- ❌ **Discrimination**: Prejudice based on identity or background
- ❌ **Trolling**: Deliberate provocation or disruption
- ❌ **Insults**: Derogatory or demeaning language
- ❌ **Threats**: Intimidation or threatening behavior
- ❌ **Privacy Violations**: Sharing private information without consent
- ❌ **Doxxing**: Revealing personal information publicly
- ❌ **Spam**: Unsolicited or repetitive messages
- ❌ **Malicious Content**: Viruses, malware, or harmful code

## 🔍 Enforcement Responsibilities

### Community Leaders

Community leaders are responsible for:

- Clarifying and enforcing our standards
- Creating welcoming and inclusive spaces
- Taking appropriate and fair corrective action
- Responding to reports promptly and professionally

### Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all community spaces, and also applies when
an individual is officially representing the community in public spaces.
Examples of representing our community include:

- Using an official e-mail address
- Posting via an official social media account
- Acting as an appointed representative at an online or offline event

## 📝 Reporting Guidelines

### How to Report

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, please report it:

1. **Email (Preferred)**
   - 📧 conduct@vcomm.io
   - PGP Key: Available on request
   - Response time: < 48 hours

2. **GitHub**
   - Create a private issue with the `conduct` label
   - Mention @security-team
   - Provide details and evidence

3. **Discord**
   - DM a moderator
   - Use the `#report-abuse` channel
   - Include screenshots if applicable

### What to Include

Your report should include:

- Your contact information (optional)
- Names of those involved
- Description of the incident
- Date and time of the incident
- Any relevant evidence (screenshots, logs, messages)
- Your desired outcome

### Confidentiality

All reports are treated confidentially. We will:

- Protect your identity if requested
- Only share information with necessary parties
- Maintain privacy throughout the process

---

## ⚖️ Enforcement Process

### Step 1: Initial Review (0-48 hours)
- Acknowledge receipt of report
- Verify the details
- Assign investigators

### Step 2: Investigation (1-7 days)
- Gather evidence
- Interview involved parties
- Assess severity
- Document findings

### Step 3: Determination (7-14 days)
- Decide on appropriate action
- Notify all parties
- Implement consequences
- Document resolution

### Step 4: Follow-up (Ongoing)
- Monitor for recurrence
- Check on affected parties
- Update policies if needed
- Close report

---

## 🚨 Consequences

### Severity Levels

#### Level 1: Minor Offense
- First warning
- Private conversation
- Educational resources provided

#### Level 2: Moderate Offense
- Temporary suspension (7-30 days)
- Public statement (if appropriate)
- Mandatory training

#### Level 3: Serious Offense
- Permanent ban from community
- Public announcement
- Legal action if necessary

### Permanent Ban

Community leaders may ban anyone who:

- Violates the Code of Conduct repeatedly
- Engages in harassment or discrimination
- Threatens or intimidates others
- Shares private information without consent
- Causes significant harm to the community

### Appeals Process

If you believe a consequence was unfair:

1. Submit an appeal to appeals@vcomm.io within 14 days
2. Explain why you believe the decision was incorrect
3. Provide any additional evidence
4. Our appeals committee will review within 7 days

## 💬 Communication Guidelines

### Constructive Feedback

When giving feedback:

- Be specific and actionable
- Focus on the work, not the person
- Provide context and examples
- Offer suggestions for improvement
- Assume positive intent

When receiving feedback:

- Listen with an open mind
- Ask clarifying questions
- Thank the person for their input
- Consider the feedback carefully
- Respond professionally

### Conflict Resolution

If you have a conflict:

1. **Direct Communication**: Try to resolve directly first
2. **Mediation**: Request help from a community leader
3. **Formal Process**: File a report if needed
